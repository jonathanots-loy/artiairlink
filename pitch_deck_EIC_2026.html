60–80% of crop losses in Sub-Saharan Africa come from diseases that could have been treated 3–4 weeks earlier — if farmers could afford to know what was wrong.

The Solution

A €150 drone + a free app = 90-second diagnosis, offline.

01

Multi-Index RGB Engine

6 vegetation indices (ExG, GRVI, GLI, TGI, VARI + AVI composite) calibrated per crop and growth stage. Runs on device CPU — zero cloud, zero sensor.

02

On-Device AI — Phase 2

MobileNetV3-Large via TensorFlow.js (≤16MB, target ≥85% accuracy), bootstrapped on PlantVillage. Requires a Belgian field-data partnership to adapt the model to local crop varieties — outreach already initiated with HE2B, awaiting response. Scaling this into a full research partnership is part of the EIC funding ask. Today, Phase 1 ships a rule-based diagnostic engine, not a trained classifier.

03

Offline-First PWA

Service worker pre-caches all assets. Works at 2G or zero connectivity. No app store — instant install from browser. Designed for rural DRC.
